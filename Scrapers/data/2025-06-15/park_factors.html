Park Factors

<< 6/14 June 15, 2025 6/16 >>

Last Updated: 12:06 PM

Another mix of temperatures is in store for Sunday with weather ranging from high 50s to mid 80s. Some of the best conditions are in Kansas City where it will be close to 80° with light wind angling slightly out to left. Kauffman Stadium still rates on the negative side for home runs due to the large outfield, but the park can be good for hits in the yard when conditions are decent.

It will be in the 80s for Giants / Dodgers this evening, which is warmer than the typical game in LA. While the wind is blowing out, this shouldn't be considered that notable as the same wind pattern is present for nearly every game at Dodger Stadium. The venue is rated positive for both runs and homers on Sunday.

Fenway Park continues its run of unseasonably cool weather with high 50s expected in Boston this afternoon for Yankees / Red Sox. The lower temps and higher air pressure have the hitter-friendly venue once again farther down the slate than usual, especially for this time of year.

The wind is blowing in again at Wrigley Field, and although it's only single digits, the direction is important as wind speed can vary quite a bit in Chicago and have more of an impact beyond what the forecasted speed may imply.

Roof status is uncertain for Milwaukee this afternoon where it will be in the 60s with cloudy weather. It's assumed open below until hearing otherwise.

Christian Vázquez Bio

  • Fullname: Christian Rafael Vázquez
  • Nickname: Colo
  • Born: 8/21/1990 in Bayamon, Puerto Rico
  • Draft: 2008, Boston Red Sox, Round: 9, Overall Pick: 292
  • High School: Puerto Rico Baseball Academy, Gurabo, PR
  • Debut: 7/09/2014
Christian Rafael Vázquez
Christian Rafael Vázquez (pronounced VOSS-kez)...Instagram: @1990colo (1990 for his birth year and 'colo' for color as reference to his red hair color)...Has a wife, Gabriela (married in 2018)...The couple has three sons, Diego (born in November 2019), Daniel (born April 2022) and Dilan (born September 2024)...Graduated from Puerto Rico Baseball Academy High School in 2008...On Jan. 6, 2018 (Three Kings Day), he helped hand out toys to children in some of the island’s poor communities...In January 2018, helped deliver nearly 10 tons of supplies to aid Hurricane Maria recovery efforts...The JetBlue plane cargo included medical supplies and vaccines, water filtration systems, first aid kits, flashlights and diapers...In Caguas, he helped distribute food, water and supplies to nearly 300 families, as well as baseball equipment to roughly 100 children...Signed autographs and posed for pictures with fans at Red Sox Winter Weekend in 2015, ‘16, ‘18 and ‘20...Along with his wife, spearheaded the Red Sox’s Players Favorite Things basket auction in 2021 that benefitted The BASE...Played Minor League ball with Twins assistant GM Alex Hassan while at Short-A Lowell in Boston's organization...Hassan shared a story that one day he sat behind Vázquez on the bus during a team road trip and Hassan could see Vázquez's laptop on AOL Instant Messenger in the seat in front of him; Vázquez's screen name was mlboneday...Chose uniform number eight for Minnesota because he wanted seven (retired: Joe Mauer) and nine (worn by Trevor Larnach), so settled in the middle...He wore number seven for Boston and number nine for Houston...Enjoys Starbucks coffee (lattes) and passes on other coffee shop options...Participated in 2023 and 2024 TwinsFest at Target Field...If he wasn't a baseball player, he would be an athletic trainer.

2024

Was on Twins' Opening Day roster as one of club's two catchers, splitting time with Ryan Jeffers for majority of season...Played one out at third base on April 12 at Detroit...Twins removed Carlos Correa due to injury, sliding Kyle Farmer to shortstop and Willi Castro to pitcher to close out an 8-2 loss to Tigers...First time playing third base since July 2021 with Boston...Slashed just .181/.207/.252 (.459 OPS) in 48 games through June, but hit .304 (34-for-112) with five doubles, five home runs, 16 RBI and an .813 OPS in 37 games from July 1-Sept. 11...Hit third career walk-off home run on July 7 vs. Houston, a solo shot off Josh Hader in the ninth inning that gave the Twins a 3-2 victory...Made first career start at third base on July 13 at San Francisco, played all 9.0 innings and never had any fielding opportunities...Forced to play third with Jose Miranda, Royce Lewis and Kyle Farmer all on IL, and Correa not fully healthy...Twins also carried three catchers at that time...Hit .317 (13-for-41) with one double, three home runs, seven RBI, seven runs scored, a .349 on-base percentage, a .561 slugging percentage and a .910 OPS in 14 July games...Played in 900th career game on Aug. 6 at Chicago-NL...Hit safely in 10 straight starts from Aug. 2-21...Hit safely in eight straight games overall during that streak from Aug. 2-17, his longest of 2024...Celebrated 10 years of Major League service on Aug. 15 with the team in Texas...His wife got a Puerto Rican band to visit the clubhouse and had his locker decorated with balloons and cookies...Hit .302 (16-for-53) with three doubles, two home runs, nine RBI and a .793 OPS in 16 August games...Placed on paternity list on Sept. 9 to attend the birth of his son; reinstated on Sept. 11...Recorded 150th career double on Sept. 11 vs. Los Angeles-AL...Played 2.0 innings at first base on Sept. 14 vs. Cincinnati in a Twins 11-1 defeat...Posted an average exit velocity of 88.5 mph, his highest since 2019 (89.4 mph)...Hit .313 (10-for-32) against off-speed pitches.

2023

Played for Team Puerto Rico during World Baseball Classic, hitting .333 (5-for-15) with two doubles, one home run, three RBI and one walk in four games...Was Twins Opening Day catcher on March 30 at Kansas City after signing three-year contract with Minnesota in December...Recorded first hit as Twin with a fifth-inning single and finished the day 2-for-4...Hit .379 (11-for-29) with a .471 on-base percentage and a .953 OPS in first nine games...Reached base safely a season-high four times on May 15 at Los Angeles-NL, going 3-for-5 with a walk...Hit first home run as Twin against former team on June 19 vs. Boston, snapping streak of 161 plate appearances to go homerless to start season...Hit safely in season-long five-straight games from Aug. 13-22...Played in 800th career game on July 26 vs. Seattle...Recorded 300th career RBI on July 29 at Kansas City...Played one inning at second base on Sept. 4 at Cleveland in a Twins 20-6 victory...Seventh career game at second base and first since July 22, 2022 vs. Toronto...Recorded 700th career hit on Sept. 12 vs. Tampa Bay, a single off Andrew Kittredge...Hit .250 (18-for-72) with five home runs and a .776 OPS vs. left-handed pitching, compared to .217 (55-for-254) with one home run and a .547 OPS vs. right-handed pitching...Posted a 1.45 second "pop time" from behind the plate, sixth best among MLB catchers...Posted a 4.09 catcher ERA, seventh best in the American League among qualified catchers and second on team (Jeffers - 3.64)...Hit .270 (10-for-37) with runners in scoring position and two outs...Hit .260 (19-for-73) with runners in scoring position...Did not appear in any of six Twins postseason games, two ALWCS vs. Toronto and four ALDS vs. Houston...Served as backup catcher to Ryan Jeffers, who started all six games.

POSTSEASON: Played in 11 games, making seven starts...Batted .281 (9-for-32) with one HR and six RBI...Hit a walk-off two-run HR in the 13th inning of Game 3 of the ALDS vs. TB.

Went 1-for-2 with 2 BB, a SB, and a game-winning HR on 6/27 vs. MIN, his first HR since 5/1/16 vs. NYY...Was his 3rd career HR, each of which was a tie-breaking 2-run shot at Fenway Park...Scored a run in a career-high 5 straight games from 6/27-7/4 (6 runs).

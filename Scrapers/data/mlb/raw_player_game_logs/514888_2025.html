Jose Carlos Altuve
Signed by Houston as a 16-year-old non-drafted free agent out of Maracay, Venezuela, on Sept. 27, 2006... the Astros Venezuelan Coordinator at the time, Pablo Torrealba, and a local Astros scout, Wolfgang Ramos, are credited with the signing of Altuve, which might be the most influential signing in Astros history...in addition to those two men, Omar López, who was the club’s Venezuelan team manager at the time, and Al Pedrique, the Astros former Special Assistant, also had strong ties to the signing...after all these years, López remains with the organization, now serving on the Astros Major League coaching staff...It took Altuve many trips to the Astros Venezuelan Academy to receive a contract offer from the club... he eventually made believers out of every staffer, but not yet the organization...Torrealba, Ramos and López all pleaded with Pedrique to visit the academy himself to take a look at Altuve...Pedrique agreed, made the trip, and came away a believer...Pedrique then made the pitch to Astros General Manager Tim Purpura, who signed off on the deal...Altuve signed for just $15,000, and according to Lopez, would have signed for free for just an opportunity to play baseball...Sixteen years after his signing, Altuve now has eight All-Star Game selections under his belt (2012, 2014- 18, 2021-22), including starting nods in 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018 and 2022...is the first player in franchise history to be named an All-Star starter five times, besting Hall of Famer Craig Biggio (1995-98), who started the All-Star Game four times in his career...IN THE COMMUNITY: Jose and his wife, Nina, have undertook several charitable initiatives in his career with the Astros, which has earned Jose three team nominations for the Roberto Clemente Award (2013, 2017, 2022), which goes to a Major League player who best represents the game of baseball through extraordinary character, community involvement, philanthropy and positive contributions, both on and off the field...Jose and Nina focus their efforts on children’s causes, which has included providing support to children with serious illnesses and providing meals to child services...Has signed three extensions with the Astros...most recently, on Feb. 7, 2024, signed a five-year deal that will keep him in Houston through 2029.

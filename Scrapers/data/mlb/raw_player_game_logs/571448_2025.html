Nolan Arenado Bio

  • Fullname: Nolan James Arenado
  • Nickname: Nado

  • Draft: 2009, Colorado Rockies, Round: 2, Overall Pick: 59

Nolan James Arenado
Attended El Toro High School in Lake Forest, Calif., where he played shortstop, catcher, and pitcher (2006-09)...His high school uniform No. 12 was retired in 2014...Was high school teammates with Matt Chapman and Austin Romine...His junior season, El Toro won the 2008 California Interscholastic Federation Southern Championship at Dodger Stadium...In 2009 as a senior shortstop, Arenado’s backup was undersized sophomore Matt Chapman, who only played shortstop when Arenado pitched...On Feb. 8, 2020, “Nolan Arenado Drive” was unveiled in his hometown of Lake Forest, leading into Lake Forest Sports Park...Signed his letter of intent to play college baseball at Arizona State...Originally selected by Colorado in thesecond round of the 2009 MLB Draft...Listed as the 17th-best catcher available by Baseball America, although was drafted as an infielder...Nolan is of Cuban (father Fernando) and part Cuban/Puerto Rican (mother Millie) descent, but does not speak Spanish...Visited Cuba for the first time with his family and friends after the 2016 season...Nolan is the second of three sons. His older brother, Fernando Jr., goes by his middle name, Cousy (named in honor Boston Celtics guard Bob Cousy)...His younger brother, Jonah, was selected by San Francisco in the 16th round of 2013 MLB Draft and played seven Minor League seasons (2013-19) as a corner infielder...His first cousin, Joshua Fuentes played three seasons as an infielder for the Rockies (2019-21)...Threw a perfect game in Little League, although credits his right fielder for throwing out a batter at first base after hitting a ball to right in the final inning...Lockered beside NFL quarterback Russell Wilson while both were in playing in the Rockies Minor League organization together...Shoots left-handed when playing basketball...Regularly plays wiffle ball with his brothers and cousins during offseasons. Contacted his bat company, Old Hickory, and ordered his model in a Wiffle Ball bat (Old Hickory model 28NA-Wiffle) with his name on it...Grew up a Los Angeles Dodgers fan and attended countless games at Dodger Stadium with his family. Was a fan of outfielder Shawn Green...Partners with Joe Harden and family friend Klay Thompson to produce Diamond and Key Wine, based in Napa, CA...Requested and studied past game footage of third basemen Aramis Ramírez, Álex Rodríguez, Adrián Beltré and Scott Rolen. He met Rolen at Busch Stadium in August 2019 during Scott’s Cardinals Hall of Fame induction...As part of National Bullying Prevention Month in 2018, lent voice in the ongoing Shred Hate PSA effort, a joint program between ESPN, MLB & X Games striving to end in-school bullying by encouraging youth to choose kindness.

Bryce Aron Max Harper
Bryce Aron Max Harper...Married Kayla Varner on Dec. 16, 2016; has one son, Krew Aron, and two daughters, Brooklyn Elizabeth and Kamryn Ray...In 2013, established Harper’s Heroes, a foundation that assists families affected by childhood cancer...Was featured on the cover of Sony’s MLB The Show 19 video game...Earned his GED after just two years at Las Vegas High School (Nev.)...Attended College of Southern Nevada...Was featured on the cover of Sports Illustrated in June 2009...His brother Bryan was a left-handed pitcher who pitched in the Minor Leagues from 2011-18...At the age of 17, hit .443 (101-for-228), 23 2B, four 3B, 31 HR, 98 RBI, 39 BB and 20 SB in 66 games for the College of Southern Nevada in the Scenic West Athletic Conference...As the youngest player in the SWAC, posted a .526 OBP and .987 SLG…Led the conference in a number of offensive categories…Was named 2010 SWAC Player of the Year and also earned First-Team SWAC All-Conference status...His 31 HR in 2010 set a College of Southern Nevada single-season mark…In 2009, batted .626 with 14 HR, 55 RBI and 36 SB in 39 games for Las Vegas High School and became the first high school sophomore ever named a Pre-Season First-Team All-American by Baseball America...In 2008, hit .590 with 11 HR and 67 RBI in 38 games as a freshman...Was a member of USA Baseball’s 2009 18U Team that went 8-0 to claim the COPABE Pan Am AAA Championships in Barquisimeto, VZ...Also played in the 2009 AFLAC All-American Game (2009 Jackie Robinson Award presented by AFLAC), the Area Code Games (2008 All-Area Code Team) and International High School Power Showcase Home Run Derby (2009 Ryan Howard Award)...Ranked first nationally in the final 2010 Perfect Game class rankings...Played in 2009 Perfect Game All-American Classic and 2008 PG Junior National Showcase...Signed by Mitch Sokol.

2024

Won his fourth career Silver Slugger Award and finished sixth in NL MVP voting...Became the fourth player in MLB history to win a Silver Slugger at three different positions (OF, 1B, DH), joining Miguel Cabrera, Albert Pujols and Al Oliver...In his first full season at first base, named a 2024 Rawlings Gold Glove Award finalist (also a 2019 finalist in RF)...Named All-MLB Second Team...Ranked third in the NL in 2B (42), fourth in XBH (72), SLG (.525) and OPS (.898), fifth in OBP (.373), eighth in AVG (.285) and fWAR (5.2), tied for eighth in BB (76) and tied for ninth in HR (30)...Ranked second among NL first basemen with eight outs above average and tied for third among NL 1B with five defensive runs saved...Had a 1.122 OPS with RISP (148 PA), a 1.407 OPS with RISP and two outs (48 PA) and a 2.056 OPS with the bases loaded (nine PA, 13 RBI – two GS/two 2B/BB)...His 42 doubles matched his single-season career high from 2021...Received most votes in the NL for his eighth career All-Star selection and was the third Phillies first baseman to earn a fan-elected start (Rose ’81-82, Kruk ’93); first Phillie with monthly honor in consecutive months since Ryan Howard, Aug.-Sept. 2006...Walked twice on June 9 vs. NYM at London Stadium for 1,000th career BB, is the 14th player in MLB history with 1,000 BB, 1,000+ R and 300+ HR in under 1,600 games...Reached base safely in 21 straight games from June 3-24 and went 21 straight PA without a strikeout from June 15-21;
